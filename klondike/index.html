What is Klondike Solitaire?

Klondike is the single-player card game most people simply call "Solitaire" — the one that shipped with Windows for decades. You deal 28 cards into seven tableau piles, keep the rest in a stock, and try to move all 52 cards up to four foundation piles, one per suit, in order from Ace to King.

Our version is truly unlimited: deal a fresh game with one click, drag and drop or tap to auto-move, undo as much as you like, and play without signing in.

How to play (60-second version)

  1. Build the foundations up by suit, Ace → King.
  2. Build the tableau down in alternating colours (red on black, black on red).
  3. Move a single card or a properly-ordered run between tableau piles.
  4. Only a King (or a run led by a King) can go onto an empty column.
  5. Tap the deck to draw; when it's empty, tap again to recycle.

Frequently asked questions

Is Klondike Solitaire free?
Yes — completely free, supported by ads, with no login.
Can I drag the cards?
Yes. Drag a card or run to place it, or just tap a card to send it to the best spot automatically.
Is there a daily puzzle?
Yes — the Daily Challenge deals everyone the same game each day and tracks your streak.
Is every deal winnable?
Most random Klondike deals are solvable, but not all — that's part of the classic game. Hit "New deal" any time.
