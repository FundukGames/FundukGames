How to play FreeCell

FreeCell is an open-information Solitaire: all 52 cards are dealt face up, so every game is a puzzle you can reason through. The goal is to build the four foundations from Ace to King.

The layout

  • Eight columns hold all 52 cards, face up, from the start.
  • Four free cells (top left) each store a single card.
  • Four foundations (top right) build up by suit, Ace → King.

The rules

  • Build columns down in alternating colours.
  • A free cell holds one card of any kind.
  • Any card may move onto an empty column.
  • You can move a run of several cards at once only if you have the space to do it one card at a time: the limit is (1 + free cells) × 2(empty columns).

Controls

  • Drag a card or run to place it.
  • Tap a card to auto-move it — foundation first, otherwise a sensible column or free cell.
  • Undo reverses your last move.

Winning strategy

  1. Keep free cells empty. Each occupied cell halves your ability to move runs. Cells are temporary parking, not storage.
  2. Make an empty column early. Empty columns are even more valuable than free cells — they multiply how many cards you can shift.
  3. Dig for the Aces and twos. Get the low cards onto the foundations so they stop blocking the columns.
  4. Plan the whole sequence. Before you start a long move, count your free cells and empty columns to be sure the run will land.
  5. Don't bury what you need. Since you can see every card, look ahead before dropping a card on top of one you'll want soon.

Moving more than one card

FreeCell technically moves one card at a time, but the game lets you drag a whole run when you have the space to shuffle it through free cells and empty columns behind the scenes. The number of cards you can move at once is (1 + free cells) × 2(empty columns) — and moving onto an empty column counts one fewer empty column as a helper. So if a multi-card move is refused, it isn't a bug: you simply don't have enough free space yet. Empty a free cell or a column and the limit jumps (each empty column doubles it).

Common mistakes

  • Parking cards in every free cell. Each filled cell halves your moving power — keep them empty until you genuinely need the parking.
  • Expecting big stacks to move with no space. See the formula above; count your free cells and empty columns before attempting a long run.
  • Burying a needed low card. Every card is visible, so look ahead — don't drop a card on top of an Ace or two you'll want next.

Frequently asked questions

Is FreeCell always winnable?
Almost — the vast majority of deals are solvable, which is why FreeCell is famous as the "skill" Solitaire. Use Undo to test lines.
Why won't my stack move?
You don't have enough free cells/empty columns for that many cards at once. Free up space and try again — see "Moving more than one card" above.
