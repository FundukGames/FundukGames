Best Free Solitaire Games in 2026: Klondike, Spider, FreeCell & More

Updated July 2026

"Solitaire" is really a family of dozens of card games, and the right one for you depends on how much luck, planning and patience you want in the mix. Here's an honest tour of the classics — the big three you can play free right here (no login, no download), and the other variants worth knowing — with a quick guide to which one fits your brain.

1. Klondike — the classic everyone means by "solitaire"

Seven columns, build the foundations Ace to King, alternate colors on the tableau. It's the version Windows made universal, and it's still the best on-ramp: rounds are short, rules are muscle memory for most people, and there's real skill in deciding what to dig for. Roughly 80% of draw-one deals are winnable in theory, far fewer in practice — which keeps it interesting. Play Klondike free — draw-one, unlimited deals, daily challenge, undo and timer.

2. Spider — the marathon

Two decks, ten columns, eight full King-to-Ace runs to assemble. One-suit Spider is a relaxing tidy-up; four-suit Spider is one of the hardest common solitaires. It rewards patience and long plans — empty columns are gold, and one careless deal from the stock can bury the whole board. Play Spider free in 1, 2 or 4 suits, then raise your win rate with our Spider strategy guide.

3. FreeCell — the thinker's solitaire

Every card is dealt face up, so there's no luck of the draw once the game starts — almost every deal is winnable, and losses are your own doing. That makes FreeCell the closest thing to a pure puzzle in the family: it's all about sequencing moves through four spare cells. Play FreeCell free, and see the FreeCell strategy guide for the habits that win consistently.

4. Pyramid — quick arithmetic snacks

Clear a pyramid of cards by removing pairs that sum to 13. Rounds take a couple of minutes and much is decided by the deal, so it's more of a coffee-break scratch than a strategy session — but a satisfying one.

5. TriPeaks — the streak machine

Clear three peaks by playing cards one rank above or below your current card. Fast, generous and built around chains — long streaks feel great, which is why casual mobile games borrow the formula so often.

6. Golf — the purest "one more round"

One row of columns, one waste pile: play up or down from the top card, empty the board before the stock runs out. Minimal rules, near-instant rounds, surprisingly tactical about which column to draw down first.

7. Yukon and Forty Thieves — for veterans

Yukon plays like Klondike unchained: you can move any face-up group, even unsorted, which turns the tableau into a big untangling exercise. Forty Thieves goes the other way — two decks, single-card moves and an unforgiving stock — one of the toughest classics. Try them when the big three feel routine.

Which solitaire should you play?

  • Want the familiar classic? Klondike.
  • Want zero luck and full control? FreeCell.
  • Want a long, meaty challenge? Spider — start at 2 suits.
  • Want a two-minute break? Pyramid, TriPeaks or Golf.
