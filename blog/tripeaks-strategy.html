TriPeaks Strategy: Chain Longer, Clear More

Published July 12, 2026

TriPeaks is the sprinting solitaire: one waste pile, one rule — play any open card one rank above or below the waste top — and a deck that never refills. Every draw from the stock is a small defeat, so the whole game is squeezing maximum cards out of every waste top. These are the decisions that separate a cleared board from three stranded peaks. Practice them on our free TriPeaks.

1. Read both directions before you play

Every playable card is a fork: from a 7 you can run 8-9-10 or 6-5-4. Before the first tap, trace both directions to their end and compare — not just chain length, but what each chain uncovers. Beginners take the first legal card they see; strong players routinely play the "wrong-looking" direction because it flips more face-down cards.

2. Uncovering beats combo length

The win condition is 28 empty peak slots, not a combo counter. A three-card chain that flips two face-down cards builds your future; a six-card chain along the open bottom row can leave you rich in points and bankrupt in options. When chain and uncovering conflict, choose uncovering — the combos come back on their own once more cards are open.

3. Use the wrap — it's where big chains live

Ace and King connect: Q-K-A-2-3 is a legal run. Players trained on other card games miss K-A and A-K plays constantly, and those corner turns are exactly what stitches two medium chains into one monster. When the waste shows a Queen or a 2, actively look for the wrap before drawing.

4. Balance the three peaks

Face-down cards only flip when both cards covering them are gone — so a peak you've ignored all game has no open cards left to work with, and its summit needs exact waste tops you no longer control. Spread your removals: when two plays are otherwise equal, take the one on the most neglected peak. An untouched peak in the endgame is usually a loss.

5. Ration the deck

Our version deals 23 stock cards and allows no redeals — the deck is a countdown timer. Exhaust every legal play before each draw, and in the late game count: cards left in stock versus face-down cards still buried. When the stock is nearly dry, spend your "held" duplicates (two open 9s, say) — the second copy's value as a re-entry point expires with the deck.

6. Hold a twin as a lifeline

Speaking of duplicates: when two copies of the same rank are open, playing one keeps the other as a guaranteed re-entry after your next draw. It's the closest thing TriPeaks has to insurance — don't cash both into the same chain unless it clears a peak.

Common mistakes

  • Auto-playing the first legal card — the fork you didn't check was usually better.
  • Chasing the combo counter — it's a bragging stat, not the win condition.
  • Forgetting suits don't matter — any 5 plays on any 6 or 4; scan all open cards, not just the tidy-looking ones.
  • Drawing reflexively — every draw resets your combo and burns the clock. Look twice first.
