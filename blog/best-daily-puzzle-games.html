10 Best Daily Puzzle Games to Play in 2026

Updated July 2026

The daily puzzle is the healthiest habit in gaming: one shared challenge, a few minutes, compare with friends, done. Here are the ten dailies we think are most worth your morning in 2026 — ranked by how good the puzzle itself is, not just how famous — with a free unlimited version noted wherever one exists, for the days one round isn't enough. (Curious why these games are so sticky? We wrote about the psychology of dailies separately.)

1. Wordle

Still the king. Six guesses, one five-letter word, and a scoreboard made of colored squares that the whole internet understands. The puzzle is simple, but the meta-game — opening words, elimination logic — has real depth. Want more? See our 12 best games like Wordle.

2. NYT Connections

Sixteen words, four hidden groups, and at least one trap designed to bait you. The best dailies make you feel clever and foolish in the same minute; Connections is the genre's master of it. Alternatives: games like Connections.

3. LinkedIn Queens

Place one crown per row, column and color region, no two touching. It's proper constraint logic in a 60-second package, and the reason half of LinkedIn is late to standup. Unlimited version: CrownGrid — free, no login, every puzzle solvable without guessing.

4. LinkedIn Tango

Fill the grid with two symbols: no three in a row, balanced rows and columns, = and ✕ clues. Binary logic that starts trivial and sneaks up on you. Unlimited version: SunMoon, same rules with suns and moons — plus our solving guide.

5. LinkedIn Zip

Draw one line through every cell, hitting the numbers in order. The youngest LinkedIn daily and the most tactile — the last three cells clicking into place is peak puzzle dopamine. Unlimited version: Trail; see also more games like Zip.

6. NYT Strands

A themed word search where every letter gets used and one "spangram" spans the board. Gentler than Connections, great for word people who find crosswords too committal.

7. The Mini (NYT)

A 5×5 crossword you can finish before the kettle boils. The speedrun timer turned it into a competitive sport; friendly leagues form around beating each other's times.

8. Waffle

Wordle's cousin with a twist: six words are already on the board, scrambled — swap letters into place in as few moves as possible. Adds a planning layer the original doesn't have.

9. Daily Sudoku

The evergreen. Any sudoku site (ours included) runs a daily grid, and the format never ages because the logic is bottomless — from scanning basics to X-Wings. Play unlimited Sudoku or brush up with our 8 techniques to solve faster.

10. A daily solitaire deal

Card games do dailies too: one shared deal, one attempt to win it. Our Klondike, Spider and FreeCell each run a daily challenge alongside unlimited play — a longer, chewier ritual than the word games.

Build your own daily routine

Most players stack two or three dailies: a word one (Wordle or Strands), a logic one (Queens, Tango or Zip) and something longer for the commute (sudoku or a solitaire deal).
