What is Mahjong Solitaire?

Mahjong Solitaire (also known as Shanghai or Taipei) is the single-player tile-matching game built from the beautiful tiles of the four-player classic. 144 tiles are stacked into the traditional turtle; you remove them two at a time by matching identical free tiles — tiles with nothing on top and at least one open side. Dots, bamboo and characters must match exactly; the four 🌸 flowers match each other, as do the four 🍂 seasons.

Most mahjong sites shuffle blindly, so a share of their deals are mathematically dead on arrival. Ours are dealt by simulating a legal disassembly backwards, so a winning line always exists. Play unlimited boards or the shared Daily with streaks.

The rules

  1. Tap two identical free tiles to remove them.
  2. A tile is free when no tile rests on it and its left or right side is open.
  3. 🌸 flowers match any flower; 🍂 seasons match any season; everything else matches exactly.
  4. Clear all 144 tiles to win. Hints, shuffle and unlimited undo are at hand.

Quick strategy tips

  • Work the top and the long row first. The peak tile and the wings lock more tiles than anything else — free them early.
  • Count to four. Each tile face appears four times. When you see three of a kind free, be careful which pair you take — the wrong pick buries the fourth.
  • Prefer matches that open matches. Two free tiles that unlock stacked rows beat an easy pair on the edge.
  • Don't burn the shuffle early. Undo is smarter — it keeps the board's guaranteed-solvable line intact.
