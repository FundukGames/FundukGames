How to play SunMoon

SunMoon is a binary logic puzzle solved by pure deduction. Here are the rules and the techniques that crack every board.

The rules

  • Every cell holds one of two symbols: a ☀️ sun or a 🌙 moon.
  • No three identical symbols may sit next to each other in a row or column.
  • Each row and each column contains an equal number of ☀️ and 🌙.
  • A = clue between two cells means they are the same; an ✕ clue means they differ.
  • Some cells are given at the start (locked).

Controls

  • Tap an empty cell → ☀️. Tap again → 🌙. Tap a third time → clear.
  • Given (locked) cells can't be changed.
  • Cells that break a rule are outlined in red.

Solving strategies

  1. Avoid triples. If two identical symbols sit side by side, the cells on both ends must be the opposite symbol.
  2. Fill the gaps. A single empty cell between two identical symbols must be the opposite (otherwise you'd make three in a row).
  3. Count to the balance. Once a row or column has all of one symbol placed (N/2), every remaining cell in that line must be the other symbol.
  4. Use the clues. An ✕ forces opposites, an = forces a matching pair — chain these with the no-triple rule for quick wins.

A worked example

Say a row on a 6-wide board starts ☀️ ☀️ and the rest is blank (three suns, three moons per row). The two suns force the third cell to be 🌙 — three suns in a row is illegal. That moon plus the balance rule then constrains the rest: you still need one sun and two moons across the last three cells, and the no-three rule blocks any run from forming. Chaining "no triples" with "balance" like this resolves most of a board before you even use the = / ✕ clues.

Common mistakes

  • Ignoring balance until the end. The equal-count rule applies from move one — as soon as a line has all its suns (or moons) placed, every remaining cell is forced.
  • Misreading clues. = means the two cells match; ✕ means they differ. Swapping them cascades into contradictions.
  • Filling fast and making a triple. Three identical symbols in a line is always illegal — check both directions before you commit a cell.

Frequently asked questions

What kind of puzzle is SunMoon?
It's a Tango-style binary logic puzzle — the "two-symbol" grid family, with no-three-in-a-row, balanced lines, and equals/opposite clues.
Do I ever need to guess?
No — every board is generated to be solvable by logic alone, and rule-breaking cells are outlined in red so you can backtrack.
Is there a daily puzzle?
Yes, plus unlimited random boards — free and with no login.
