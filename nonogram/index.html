What is a nonogram?

A nonogram (also called picture cross, Picross or griddlers) is a grid-based logic puzzle. The numbers beside each row and above each column tell you the lengths of the runs of filled cells in that line, in order — for example, "3 1" means a block of three filled cells, then a gap of at least one, then a single filled cell. Cross-referencing rows against columns fills the grid one deduction at a time.

Unlike puzzles locked to one per day, this nonogram is unlimited — pick 5×5, 10×10 or 15×15 and tap "New puzzle" any time, or take the shared Daily Challenge and build a streak.

The rules

  1. The clue numbers give the filled runs in each row and column, in order.
  2. Between two runs there is at least one empty cell.
  3. Fill the cells that must be filled; mark ✕ where you know it's empty.
  4. Complete every line correctly and the hidden picture appears.

Quick solving tips

  • Start with big numbers. A 10-clue in a 10-wide row fills the whole line; an 8-clue pins down the middle 6 cells no matter where the run sits.
  • Use the overlap trick. Slide each run as far left and as far right as it can go — cells covered in both extremes are certainly filled.
  • Mark the ✕s. Knowing a cell is empty is as valuable as filling one — it splits lines into smaller, easier sections.
  • Finished lines first. When a line's runs are all placed, ✕ the rest and watch the crossing lines simplify.
