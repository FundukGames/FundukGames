What is Pyramid Solitaire?

Pyramid is one of the great classic solitaires — sometimes called Solitaire 13 or Tut's Tomb. The tableau is a 7-row pyramid of 28 cards; the rest of the deck waits in the stock. You remove cards in pairs that sum to 13: Ace counts 1, Jack 11, Queen 12 — and a King is 13 by itself, so it clears with a single tap. A pyramid card is only playable while nothing overlaps it, which is where the planning comes in: dig toward the pairs you need before the stock runs dry.

Deal unlimited games or play the shared Daily Challenge with streaks. Undo generously — it's a thinking game, not a punishment.

The rules

  1. Tap two open cards whose values add to 13 to remove them (A=1, J=11, Q=12).
  2. Kings clear alone — one tap.
  3. A pyramid card is open when no card overlaps it; the top of the waste pile is always open.
  4. Tap the deck to draw a card; the deck can be recycled twice.
  5. Remove all 28 pyramid cards to win — leftovers in the stock don't matter.

Frequently asked questions

Is Pyramid Solitaire free?
Yes — completely free, supported by ads. No download, no login.
Is every deal winnable?
No — like most solitaires, some deals can't be cleared even with perfect play. That's part of the game; undo and the two redeals give you plenty of room to try.
What happens to cards left in the stock?
Nothing — you win the moment the pyramid itself is empty.
Is there a daily deal?
Yes — everyone gets the same daily deal, and clearing it builds your streak.

Quick strategy tips

  • Count to 13 from the top down. Before pairing, check which buried cards you'll need later — don't spend a 6 the pyramid's 7 depends on.
  • Kings are free tempo. Clear them the moment they open; they never help anyone else.
  • Dig evenly. A lopsided pyramid locks its own middle; keep both flanks moving.
  • Watch the four-of-a-kind budget. Each rank has exactly four cards — if two 8s are gone and both 5s are buried under each other, that pairing lane is dead.
