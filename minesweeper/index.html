What makes this Minesweeper different?

Standard Minesweeper has a dirty secret: some boards force you to guess. You play perfectly for three minutes, reach two identical squares, and lose to a coin-flip. Our generator plays every board with a logic solver before you see it — using the same deductions a human can make — and rejects or repairs any board that requires luck. If the board is on your screen, there is always a safe, findable move.

Three difficulty levels (9×9, 16×16 and 24×16), unlimited boards, and a shared Daily Challenge with streaks. Your first dig is always safe and opens a working area.

The rules

  1. Dig cells to reveal them. A number counts the mines in its 8 neighbors.
  2. Flag the cells you've proven to be mines (right-click or long-press).
  3. Tap a number whose flags are all placed to open its remaining neighbors.
  4. Reveal every safe cell to win. Dig a mine and it's game over — but you can retry the board.

Frequently asked questions

Is this Minesweeper free?
Yes — completely free, supported by ads. No download, no login.
What does "no-guess" mean?
Every board is checked by a solver that only uses human-style logic. If the solver would need to guess, the board is repaired until it doesn't. You will never face a 50/50.
Can my first click hit a mine?
No — the first dig is always safe and always opens an area to work from.
Is there a daily puzzle?
Yes — a shared 16×16 daily board with streaks, plus unlimited random boards.

Quick solving tips

  • Satisfied numbers free their neighbors. If a "1" already touches a flag, every other cell around it is safe — open them with a chord tap.
  • Count the tight fits. If a "3" has exactly three covered neighbors, all three are mines — flag them.
  • Learn the 1-2 pattern. A 1 next to a 2 along a wall: the mine of the 1 overlaps the 2's pair, forcing the cell beyond the 2 to be a mine.
  • Endgame: count mines. The 🚩 counter tells you how many mines remain — when it matches the covered cells, flag them all.
