How to play Snake

You know the drill — eat, grow, don't crash. Here's how this version handles, what the Daily maze is, and the habits that separate a 12-nut run from a 40-nut one.

The rules

  • The snake moves constantly; you only steer. Each 🌰 nut eaten adds a body segment and a small speed boost (capping at double speed).
  • Running into the border, a maze wall or your own body ends the run.
  • Score = nuts eaten. Classic mode tracks your all-time best; the Daily maze tracks a separate best.
  • On the Daily maze, eating 15 nuts keeps your streak.

Controls

  • Keyboard: arrow keys or WASD; Space pauses.
  • Touch: swipe on the board in the direction you want to go; tap to start.
  • Turns are queued — you can input the next two turns in advance and they'll execute one tick apart. This is the secret to tight corners at high speed.
  • You can never reverse straight into your own neck; that input is ignored.
  • Switching tabs auto-pauses.

Survival strategy

  1. Sweep in lanes. Move in orderly S-shaped sweeps rather than random darts — your body stays in predictable lines you won't box yourself into.
  2. Take the long way. Early on, the direct route to a nut is fine; past twenty segments, approach from a side that leaves your escape open.
  3. Count the pocket. Before entering an enclosed area, estimate whether your tail will have vacated the exit by the time you need it. Tail cells free up one per tick.
  4. Pre-queue double turns. A quick left-then-up entered together executes as a clean corner — waiting to see the first turn land is how high-speed runs die.
  5. Learn the daily walls slowly. Your first daily run is a scouting lap; the layout stays the same all day.

Common mistakes

  • Chasing every nut greedily. A nut flush against your own tail or a maze block is a trap until you shrink the risk with a wider approach.
  • Steering late. The speed cap is double the start pace — by then, if you haven't queued the turn, it's already too late.
  • Circling the center. The middle is where tails accumulate; the edges are your friend when long.
  • Panic reversing. Reverse inputs are ignored — mashing them costs you the moment you needed for a real turn.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Daily maze the same for everyone?
Yes — the walls are generated from the date, so all players share the same field. 15 nuts keeps your streak alive.
Does the snake wrap around the edges?
No — the border is solid, classic rules. What kills you is always visible.
Can I pause?
Yes — Space or the Pause button, and the game pauses automatically when the tab loses focus.
